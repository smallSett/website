    cs2220

    An introduction to the theory and development aspects of a high-level programming language. The course covers programming methodologies, control structures, predefined and user defined functions, input/output streams, control structures, logical expressions, enumeration, repetition, multidimensional array and string manipulation, structures, searching, sorting techniques, and advanced input/output. Program analysis, design, development, and testing are emphasized

    A continuation of Computer Science I to include advanced programming techniques including classes and data abstractions, inheritance and composition, pointers, virtual functions, overloading, exception handling, and recursion. Students analyze, design, implement, and test complex programs. Prerequisite: CS 2250.

    Broad perspectives and overviews of computer science concepts are presented. This course helps students prepare for more in-depth coverage in higher-level courses. Topics include introduction to algorithms, computer system organization and operation, digital circuits design, introduction to theory of computing, language and grammar, network, software systems and security, image and computer graphics, issues, impact and opportunities in the field of computer science.
